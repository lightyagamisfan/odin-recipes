Alaska Salmon Bake with Pecan Crunch Coating

Description

The Alaska Salmon Bake with Pecan Crunch Coating is a delicious and unique dish that combines the rich flavors of wild-caught Alaska salmon with the crispy, nutty texture of a pecan-based crust. The salmon, known for its firm texture and natural, slightly sweet taste, is first coated with a mixture of crushed pecans, breadcrumbs, and seasonings, which is then baked to perfection. The result is a golden-brown, crispy exterior with a tender and juicy salmon interior.

This dish highlights the delicate balance between the savory, mild taste of the salmon and the rich, slightly sweet crunch of the pecans, making it a flavorful and satisfying meal. The pecans not only add a wonderful texture but also bring a subtle nuttiness that complements the fish beautifully. Often paired with a side of vegetables or a light salad, the Alaska Salmon Bake with Pecan Crunch Coating offers a healthy yet indulgent option, ideal for seafood lovers or anyone seeking a gourmet twist on a classic fish dish.

Ingredients

  1. 4 Alaska salmon fillets (skin-on or skinless, depending on preference)
  2. 1 cup pecans, finely chopped or crushed
  3. 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (preferably panko for a lighter, crunchier texture)
  4. 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional for extra flavor)
  5. 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)
  6. 1 tsp garlic powder
  7. 1 tsp onion powder
  8. 1/2 tsp smoked paprika (or regular paprika)
  9. 1/4 tsp salt
  10. 1/4 tsp black pepper
  11. 1 tbsp olive oil (or melted butter, for brushing)
  12. Lemon wedges (for serving, optional)
  13. Fresh herbs (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375째F (190째C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly grease it with olive oil.
  2. Prepare the Pecan Coating: In a shallow bowl or plate, combine the finely chopped pecans, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan (if using), garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Mix well.
  3. Prepare the Salmon: Pat the salmon fillets dry with a paper towel. Brush each fillet with a thin layer of olive oil (or melted butter) to help the coating adhere.
  4. Coat the Salmon: Press each salmon fillet, flesh-side down, into the pecan mixture, gently pressing so that the coating sticks. Make sure the top of each fillet is evenly coated with the pecan mixture.
  5. Bake: Place the coated salmon fillets on the prepared baking sheet. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and the pecan crust is golden and crispy. The internal temperature of the salmon should reach 145째F (63째C).
  6. Serve: Remove from the oven and let the salmon rest for a couple of minutes. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley (optional) and serve with lemon wedges for extra brightness.
